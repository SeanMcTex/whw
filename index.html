Boots — when to buy & break in
The sweet spot
Buy now (5 months out) — ideal timing. 8–12 weeks of active use is the break-in target. Wear them on every training walk from day one; don't save them. By 4–6 weeks out they should feel like old friends. If they still don't fit well at that point, you still have time to switch.
Don't worry about wearing them out
Quality hiking boots last 500–1,000 miles. Your break-in adds maybe 50–100 miles — a rounding error on their lifespan. The real danger is buying too close to departure, not getting enough wear on them, and destroying your feet on Day 1.
What to look for
Waterproof (Gore-Tex or equivalent, not just water-resistant — the distinction matters enormously in October Highland bog). Ankle support. A genuine hiking boot, not a trail runner. Pair with merino wool socks; use the same socks during break-in as you'll wear on the trail.
October conditions
Rain and mud are near-certain. Rannoch Moor on Day 5 is a legitimate bog. Waterproofness is not optional — if your boots get wet inside, you'll have blisters within hours. Gaiters help too.
Training
The real challenge
Not any single day — it's the cumulative multi-day fatigue. Training with back-to-back long walks on weekends is the single most useful thing you can do. Your legs on Day 5 will feel very different from your legs on Day 1.
Mileage targets
Now–June: 5–8 miles, 3x/week. July: 10–12 miles long day. August: 15–18 miles long day + 2–3 day back-to-back hike. Your hardest day on the itinerary is 18.5 miles (Day 5) — you want to have exceeded that in training before you leave.
San Antonio is flat — address that
The WHW has ~3,500m total elevation gain. Actively seek out hills in training, or supplement with squats, lunges, calf raises, and step-ups. The climbs (Conic Hill, Devil's Staircase, Mamore Ridge) will bite if your legs aren't prepared for ascent and descent.
Train in bad weather
Don't skip rainy days. You need to know your gear works, and you need to be mentally comfortable walking in driving Scottish rain. Get comfortable with discomfort before Scotland.
Taper
2–3 weeks before departure, reduce big mileage. Arrive rested, not worn out. A final 12–15 mile confidence walk in mid-September, then coast in.
On-trail tips
Bags out by 8:30am every day
Non-negotiable. Ask each accommodation the night before where bags should be left. Label clearly — many bags are being moved each day.
Day 3 ferry — raise the ball!
At Ardleish jetty you must physically raise the signal ball to summon the ferry. Last October sailing is 18:00 from Ardlui. Don't dawdle on the Loch Lomond section that day.
Fort William dinner — book early
Restaurants need 2+ days notice. Book from the trail on Day 5 or 6 while you still have the wherewithal to think about it.
Glencoe shuttle — chase confirmation
Your booking doc marks Days 5 & 6 shuttle as "To Be Confirmed." Follow up with Mac's well before departure.
Offline maps are essential
Download the Mac's Adventure app with offline maps before you leave. Mobile signal in the Highlands is patchy and unreliable. Don't rely on data for navigation mid-trail.
